In May 2017, I heard about Tech Elevator, a coding boot camp that promised to turn me into a jr. software developer in just 14 weeks. I couldn't enroll fast enough and by January 2018, I was attending my first classes. It has been the most challenging, fulfilling, and weirdly fun thing I've ever done for myself.

I've been working as a software and web developer since mid-2015, but if you'd asked me about going that route in 2014, I couldn't have imagined myself capable of doing all of this "mathy" work. I'd been dabbling in HTML and CSS for years, but saw anything more complex than that as wizardry.

That's why I love teaching Women, Wine and Web Design in partnership with Tech Elevator. I've made it my mission to show more women just how creative and interesting this work can be, and that (as a general rule) developers aren't unapproachable supergeniuses, they're just people who learned how to do something new.
